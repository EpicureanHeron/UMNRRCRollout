Chrome River

Over the next few weeks we will be meeting with you and your financial leadership team to discuss resources that will help your unit transition from the paper process to Chrome River and integrate the Travel Card into your suite of payment methods.

Pre-Approvals

Pre-Approvals in Chrome River allow for individuals to plan for a trip by providing a business justification and estimate their expenses. Pre-Approvals are optional by default in Chrome River. Each college/administrative unit may decide to require a Pre-Approval in Chrome River prior to making travel arrangements. Pre-Approvals route to the individual’s supervisor for approval. This is the only approval routing that is configured in Chrome River for a Pre-Approval. Regardless of whether your unit uses Pre-Approvals or not, the system is configured so that all expense reports will route to the individual’s supervisor for approval, along with all other predefined required approvals.

  • Consider whether required Pre-Approvals are right for your college/administrative unit. We need to know your decision at least two weeks before your scheduled go-live date so it can be programmed into Chrome River.

Approvals

Here’s the order of review for Expense Report transactions:

  1. Supervisor (from HRMS, the person’s “Reports To”)
  2. Financial approver (EFS primary approver for Expenses)
  3. Certified approver (when activity hits sponsored accounts)
  4. For some rarely encountered compliance rules, there are routings to the Chief Financial Manager or Controller
  5. If over $10,000, Accounting Services

Chrome River does not allow for a pool of alternates but it does have a vacation alternate feature. The University Financial Helpline can reroute activity in Chrome River, similar to how it’s done in EFS.

Approvals cannot be customized for colleges/administrative units within Chrome River.

  • Validate that the Primary Approver in EFS for Expense Reports is who you would like the approver to be in Chrome River. Make these changes as you would any other approver updates for EFS.

Supervisors are now approving in the system instead of on paper. During the pilot some units discovered a disconnect between who they expected the supervisor to be and the supervisor as listed in HRMS.

If the relationship is not correct, please work with your HR pro and HR staff to ensure job records are updated as appropriate.

Delegates for Preparing Expense Reports

While the system offers delegate functionality, delegate use is not encouraged. The most efficient way to use the Chrome River system is for travelers/employees to create and submit their own expense reports. During our pilot we asked individuals to create their own expense reports and had 88% participation.

Overall the feedback from pilot participants was positive, acknowledging that following the best practices built in the system really does make good sense for the University. Individuals who must authorize a delegate for themselves have to log in to Chrome River and grant that access person by person. People cannot authorize themselves to be delegates for others, as can be done in EFS. If a delegate does create (or edit) an Expense Report for someone, the system will require the traveler/employee to submit their own expense report, obtaining the traveler/employee electronic signature. It’s this feature that allows us to get rid of the paper process (a major driver for implementing the new system).

Chrome River Analytics

Chrome River has a powerful analytics tool that can be made available to a very small number of people at the University. As part of this rollout, each Chief Financial Manager, Cluster Director, RRC Contact, and Department Card Administrators will be granted access to Chrome River Analytics to aid in monitoring activity, pulling activity to make projections for budgeting, and responding to inquiries from college or University leaders. On an as-needed basis, you may request this access for a few additional key staff such as your collegiate data analyst. Please include the name(s) of those who should have this access on your Checklist in the Google Drive Folder (more on that below) or email us at travel@umn.edu. Travel Card

The Travel Card is available to any employee who travels, irrespective of the number of times they travel. Not all employees can afford to pay up front for travel costs or wait for reimbursement. In addition to this convenience for employees, the Travel Card helps the University better track and contain costs. Using the card along with Chrome River speeds up the Expense Report process, since much of the required information will already be in the system by virtue of having used the card and uploading images of receipts.

  • In the next few days we will share with you additional information on ordering Travel Cards. It will include a list of current PCard and American Express cardholders within your unit and instructions on how to close accounts if necessary.
  • If you want a list of your department card administrators, request it via email to controller@umn.edu.
  • A Travel Card application (docx) must be completed for each person who needs a card. Instructions for submitting applications will also be provided to you. We encourage all travelers to apply for a Travel Card.

What’s happening with the American Express Travel Card program? This program is being eliminated. Cardholders will be contacted by Purchasing Services with details about the closure of their account and any payment arrangements. Individual colleges/administrative units and departments do not need to take any steps to close out American Express Travel Cards.
